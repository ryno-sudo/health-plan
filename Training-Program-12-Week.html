12-Week Training Program

L5/S1-Safe Recomp · 90kg · Revo Charlestown · "all muscle heads" · pairs with the 4-Week Meal Plan · May 2026

1. Strategy

The one-line version: 5 lifting days a week, every session chosen to build muscle without loading your L5/S1 disc in a compromised position. No barbell on your spine, no conventional deadlift, no loaded rounding. You'll still hit every muscle — and every head of every muscle — using machines, cables, chest-supported and lying variations that Revo Charlestown has in abundance. TRT + Mast means your recovery ceiling is high, so frequency and volume are the levers, not ego-loading a barbell.
Read this before you touch a weight: This program is built around a known L5/S1 disc issue. Every exercise below is either spine-neutral or actively decompressive. If you swap something in from memory (heavy back squats, bent barbell rows, conventional deads), you're outside the plan and taking on the exact risk it's designed to remove. When in doubt, pick the machine version.

Working around Reta injection timing

Reta goes in Sunday & Wednesday nights. Appetite and energy dip Mon–Tue and Thu–Fri; they return Saturday. So the split is arranged to put the most demanding, most-fuelled session (quad-focused legs) on Saturday, and lighter, pump-style or posterior-chain work on the low-fuel days. On any day you feel flat, use the autoregulation rule in section 5 — drop a set, not the whole session.

Cardiovascular context

Your LDL is 3.9 (flagged high) and you're on TRT + Mast. Zone-2 cardio isn't optional garnish here — it's part of the cardiac-risk management the meal plan calls out (ApoB/Lp(a) pending). The cardio prescription in section 6 is a real part of the program, not a nice-to-have.

2. L5/S1 — the rules that don't bend

Non-negotiable: if you get shooting pain down a leg, numbness, tingling in the foot, or any loss of bladder/bowel control — stop training and see a doctor. That's nerve-root territory, not soreness. This program manages a disc; it does not treat acute radiculopathy.

Every session starts here — the McGill Big 3 (5 min)

These are the spine's "brace" builders. Do them as your war-up primer before every lifting day. They protect the disc by teaching the core to lock the lumbar spine still while the hips move.

MovePrescriptionCue
Curl-up (McGill)3 holds × 8–10s per set, 2 setsHands under low back, one knee bent, lift head/shoulders a few cm only. Do NOT flatten your back to the floor.

Bracing standard for all lifts: before every working set, take a breath into your belly, brace like you're about to be lightly punched, and keep that brace for the rep. Hinge from the hips, keep the ribcage stacked over the pelvis, and never let the low back round under a load or hyperextend to finish a rep.

Decompression finisher (2 min, end of each session)

  • Dead hang from a bar, 2 × 20–30s (if grip/shoulders allow) — gentle traction.
  • Child's pose or 90/90 hip-flexor breathing, 1–2 min — resets the lumbar.
  • Skip loaded stretching of the hamstrings in end-range spinal flexion.

3. The weekly split

Five lifting days, two full rest days. Each muscle is trained roughly twice a week, and legs are split into a hamstring/glute day (low spinal demand, midweek) and a quad day (Saturday, when you're best fuelled). Upper body gets a dedicated push day, pull day, and a mixed weak-point upper day.

4. The 12-week arc

Three 4-week blocks. Same split throughout — what changes is volume, rep range and proximity to failure. Weeks build, then week 12 is a deload + retest. This is classic accumulation → intensification → realise, kept safe for the disc the whole way.

How to progress within a block — double progression

Each exercise has a rep range (say 10–15). Pick a load you can do for the bottom of the range at the target RPE. Add reps week to week until you hit the top of the range on all sets — then add the smallest load increment and drop back to the bottom of the range. Repeat. That's it. On machines and cables the jumps are small, which is exactly what a recomp on maintenance calories wants.

Intensity techniques (Block 2–3 only, machines/cables only)

  • Myo-reps / rest-pause: after a hard set, rest 15s, squeeze out 3–5 more, repeat 2–3 times. Great on leg extension, curls, lateral raises, pushdowns.
  • Drop sets: run a set to near-failure, strip ~20%, go again. Best as the last set of an isolation movement, never on a spine-loaded pattern.
  • Lengthened partials: at failure, keep pumping partial reps in the stretched position (bottom of a fly, stretch of an overhead extension). Strong hypertrophy stimulus, low joint cost.
Never use intensity techniques on the 45° back extension, hip thrust, split squat, or any hinge — failure there is where spine form breaks. Keep those clean, 1–2 reps in reserve, always.

Block-by-block collapsibles

Block 1 · Weeks 1–4 · Foundation
  • Goal: perfect the machine setups, groove the brace, build work capacity. You are deliberately leaving reps in the tank.
  • All working sets at RPE 7 — stop with ~3 clean reps left.
  • Use the workout tables in section 5 at the lower set count (skip any set marked "opt").
  • Week 4 is still a build week — don't deload yet.
  • Prioritise learning: leg press foot position, hack squat depth that your back tolerates, cable angles for the delts. Film a set occasionally to check your lumbar stays neutral.
Block 2 · Weeks 5–8 · Accumulation
  • Goal: more total sets — this block does most of the muscle-building.
  • Add the "opt" sets in the workout tables. Push to RPE 8 (~2 in reserve).
  • Introduce ONE intensity technique per session on an isolation lift (not before week 6).
  • If in the growth scenario (Reta paused, surplus): this is where the extra recovery pays — add a 4th set to primary chest/back/quad lifts.
  • Watch sleep and the gut markers from the meal plan. If bloating/sleep degrade, you're overreaching — pull volume back a notch.
Block 3 · Weeks 9–11 · Intensify
  • Goal: add load in safe rep ranges (6–12), express the muscle you've built.
  • RPE 8–9 on machines — 1–2 in reserve. The last set of isolations can touch failure.
  • Keep hinge/split-squat patterns at RPE 8 max — form over ego, always.
  • Use myo-reps / drop sets / lengthened partials freely on machines and cables.
  • By now the double-progression should have your top-set numbers meaningfully up from week 1 — that's your "top-set on key lifts" tracking metric from the meal plan.
Week 12 · Deload & Retest
  • Goal: shed fatigue, confirm progress, set up the next 12 weeks.
  • Cut every exercise to ~2 sets, keep the load but stop at RPE 6 (4 in reserve). You should leave the gym feeling fresher each day.
  • Mid-week, retest 2–3 key lifts for a top set (e.g. leg press, chest-supported row, machine chest press, lateral raise load) — record reps × load vs week 1.
  • Take Saturday-morning progress photos + waist-at-navel measure (same as the meal plan's tracking).
  • Book the follow-up bloods the meal plan lists (cystatin C eGFR, ApoB/Lp(a), Hct). Decide recomp vs growth for the next block based on the eGFR result.

Options

Autoregulation — the low-fuel-day rule

On Reta-suppressed days when you feel flat, don't grind or skip. Instead:
  • Keep the first 2 exercises of the day at full effort.
  • Cut the last set of every remaining exercise (run the "base" count, skip all "opt" sets).
  • Never compromise the hinge or split-squat form to hit a number — those are the drop-first movements.
A slightly shorter session done well beats a full session done fatigued and sloppy — especially with a disc to protect.

Zone-2 heart-rate guide: roughly 180 − age, or simply the pace where you could nose-breathe and still talk. 8. Caveats

Not medical advice. This is a training template built around the constraints you gave — an L5/S1 disc, Revo Charlestown's equipment, and full muscular coverage. Clear it with your physio or doctor before starting, especially given the disc and the peptide/hormone stack. If you have current imaging or a physio's loading guidance, their limits override anything here.
  • Disc flare protocol: if the back flares, don't push through. Drop to McGill Big 3 + walking + upper-body machines that don't involve the low back (seated chest press, pulldown, curls, pushdowns) until it settles, then rebuild the leg days from lighter loads. A flare is a detour, not a reset to zero.
  • Radicular symptoms (leg pain, numbness, foot tingling, any bladder/bowel change) → stop, see a doctor. Repeated from section 2 because it matters most.
  • The growth surplus waits for your kidneys. Don't start the +2,800 kcal growth phase until the cystatin C eGFR result confirms your kidney function (meal plan, bloodwork #1). Train the same either way; just don't bulk on an unconfirmed eGFR.
  • Hct on TRT: your 0.49 is upper-normal. Hard training + TRT can nudge it — the meal plan's 3-monthly FBC covers this. Stay well hydrated (within the plan's CKD-aware water targets).
  • Deload if: sleep drops two weeks running, joints ache persistently, top sets fall, or motivation tanks. An early deload costs you nothing; an injury costs the block.
  • Equipment gaps: if Revo lacks a specific machine, use the substitutions in each day's drop-down. The principle — spine-neutral or decompressive, all heads covered — is what to preserve, not the exact machine.
