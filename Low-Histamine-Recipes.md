# Low-Histamine Recipes & Protocols — TM7 + Slow Cooker

Swap recipes for the meal plan, built for the **Low-Histamine mode** in `roadmap.md` §7.2. Every recipe is tagged for two tiers and tells you how to run it at either one.

---

## The two tiers (and why there's no "zero")

**There is no truly zero-histamine diet.** All food carries trace histamine, cooked protein makes more the longer it sits, and your own gut flora produce histamine independent of what you eat. "No histamine" in practice means the **strict/very-low tier**: ultra-fresh, cooked-and-eaten-within-the-hour, no reheating, no fermented/aged/citrus anything. Use it as a short elimination window (2–4 weeks), then relax to Low.

| Tier | Use it for | Core rules |
|---|---|---|
| **LOW** (everyday) | Ongoing default once you've settled | Fresh protein cooked same day; freeze extras immediately; one reheat-from-frozen OK; no aged/cured/fermented/smoked; no spinach/tomato/avocado/citrus |
| **VERY-LOW / STRICT** ("closest to none") | A tight 2–4 wk elimination to read the signal cleanly | Everything in LOW **plus**: eat within ~1 hr of cooking, **no reheating at all** (freeze for a *different* day only), no leftovers, no bone/long stock, no citrus even as a squeeze, peel all veg, minimal spice (salt + fresh herbs only) |

### Three rules that matter more than any recipe
1. **Freshness of protein is everything.** Histamine forms in meat/fish from the moment it's killed and climbs with time and warmth. Buy the freshest you can, or frozen-at-peak, and cook from cold — don't let it sit at fridge temp for days.
2. **Freezing halts histamine formation; the fridge does not.** This is the whole game. Cooked protein left in the fridge 3–4 days (your current batch model) is the biggest histamine load in the plan. **Cook → portion → freeze within 30–60 min of cooking.**
3. **Cook fast, cook fresh.** Short cook times and quick methods (sear, steam, ≤2 hr stock) beat long simmers and slow-cooker holds for histamine.

## Protocol 0 — The freeze-portioning protocol (replaces "keeps 4 days in fridge")

This is the low-histamine replacement for the plan's cook-Sunday-eat-leftovers model.

1. Cook the batch (stew, stock, roast protein, rice).
2. **Start portioning within 30 min of cooking finishing** — do not let it cool for hours on the counter or sit warm in the slow cooker.
3. Divide into single-meal containers. Cool to room temp *fast* (shallow containers, or sit the container in an ice-water bath).
4. **Freeze immediately.** Label with date.
5. To eat: thaw in the fridge overnight or from frozen, and reheat **once** to steaming.
   - **LOW tier:** one reheat-from-frozen is fine.
   - **STRICT tier:** freeze portions for a *future* day only, and even then some strict responders eat only fresh-cooked — if you're chasing the cleanest signal, cook single portions fresh and skip reheating.
6. Never refreeze, never reheat twice.

> Rice, roast veg and stock freeze beautifully and are low-histamine — freeze them in portions too so a "fresh" meal is 6 minutes of assembly.

## Stocks (replace long bone broth)

Long-simmered bone broth is high-histamine/glutamate (already flagged in your plan). These short-cook versions give you the gut-soothing warm-broth ritual without the histamine load.

### 1. Short-cook meat stock — TM7
**Tier: LOW** (STRICT: use it same-day only, don't store beyond an immediate freeze). Swaps for: morning bone broth 200ml.

*Makes ~1.5 L · ~90 min mostly hands-off*

| Step | Action | Time | Temp | Speed |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Add 1 roughly-chopped carrot + 1 peeled zucchini + 1 tsp salt to bowl, chop | 3 sec | — | speed 5 |
| 2 | Add 500 g **very fresh** chicken thigh/drumstick (or fresh beef chuck), 1.2 L water, 1 bay leaf | — | — | — |
| 3 | Cook | 90 min | 100°C | speed 1 (Reverse, /soft spoon setting) |
| 4 | Strain through the basket. Discard solids or eat the meat fresh (don't store the meat if chasing strict) | — | — | — |

- **Keep it ≤2 hr total** — that's the whole point vs bone broth's 12–24 hr.
- Salt to taste at the end. No onion/garlic in week 1 (methanogen rule); add a few cooked garlic cloves from week 2 if tolerated.
- **Freeze in 200 ml portions immediately** (Protocol 0). Reheat one cup at a time.

### 2. Short-cook meat stock — Stovetop
**Tier: LOW.** Same idea without the TM7.
1. Put 500 g fresh chicken pieces + 1.2 L water + 1 carrot + 1 zucchini + 1 bay leaf + 1 tsp salt in a pot.
2. Bring to a gentle simmer, skim the foam once.
3. Simmer **90 min max**, lid ajar.
4. Strain, cool fast, **freeze in 200 ml portions within 30 min**.

## Dairy swaps (replace fermented Greek yoghurt)

Greek yoghurt is fermented = high histamine. Fresh ricotta is made by acid-curdling fresh milk — **not fermented, low-histamine**, and the TM7 makes it in ~20 min.

### 3. Fresh ricotta — TM7
**Tier: LOW** (STRICT: eat within a day of making; use fresh lemon sparingly — citrus is a mild liberator, or use the cream-of-tartar option). Swaps for: pre-bed 200 g Greek yoghurt; snack yoghurt.

*Makes ~250 g · 20 min + drain*

| Step | Action | Time | Temp | Speed |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Add 1 L fresh full-cream milk + ½ tsp salt | — | — | — |
| 2 | Heat | 15 min | 90°C | speed 2 |
| 3 | Add 2 tbsp fresh lemon juice **or** ¼ tsp cream of tartar (citrus-free/strict), stir | 10 sec | 90°C | speed 3 |
| 4 | Let stand 5 min (curds separate), then strain through a lined basket 20–30 min | — | — | — |

- Pair pre-bed with **10 g collagen** (collagen is low-histamine) stirred into a little warm water — keeps the slow-protein role of the yoghurt.
- **STRICT alternative to dairy entirely:** casein or collagen protein in water. No dairy histamine at all.

## Soups & mains (fresh, fast, freezer-friendly)

### 4. Pumpkin & carrot soup — TM7
**Tier: LOW & STRICT** (fully strict-safe — peeled veg, no liberators). Swaps for: any lunch; warm gut-friendly meal.

*Makes ~1.5 L · ~30 min*

| Step | Action | Time | Temp | Speed |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 400 g peeled kent pumpkin + 2 peeled carrots, cubed → into bowl, chop | 4 sec | — | speed 5 |
| 2 | Add 1 tbsp EVOO, sauté | 4 min | 120°C | speed 1 |
| 3 | Add 700 ml fresh short-cook stock (recipe 1) or water + 1 tsp salt, cook | 18 min | 100°C | speed 1 |
| 4 | Blend to smooth | 40 sec | — | speed 8→10 |

- Finish with EVOO (polyphenols — good for your TMAO angle) and fresh herbs, not pepper if going strict (pepper is fine at LOW).
- **Freeze in portions immediately.**

### 6. Same-day chicken & fresh-rice bowl — Stovetop / TM7 rice
**Tier: LOW & STRICT** (strict: cook and eat within the hour, no marinade-vinegar, no lemon). Swaps for: the plan's chicken/pumpkin/rice lunch bowl (which used tamari + lemon — both out for histamine).

1. **Rice (TM7):** 1 cup rinsed jasmine rice + 1.5 cups water + pinch salt → 18 min / 100°C / Reverse / speed 1 (or Varoma steam). Freeze extra in portions.
2. **Chicken:** season 200 g **fresh** chicken thigh with salt + fresh rosemary + 1 tsp EVOO (no tamari, no lemon). Pan-sear medium-high 4 min/side to 75°C internal. Slice.
3. **Veg:** steam broccolini/zucchini/carrot 4 min (TM7 Varoma or stovetop steamer).
4. Bowl up: rice + veg + chicken. Dress with **EVOO + salt + fresh herbs** (no vinegar/lemon).
   - LOW flavour add-ons: a little fresh ginger, fresh herbs, cooked garlic (wk2+).
   - STRICT: salt + EVOO + herbs only.

### 7. Fresh meat-stock risotto — TM7

## Dressings & flavour (replace tamari / vinegar)

Tamari (fermented soy), vinegar, and aged condiments are all high-histamine. Flavour comes from fresh instead.

### 8. Fresh herb & EVOO dressing — TM7
**Tier: LOW & STRICT** (strict: omit lemon).
- Handful fresh parsley/basil + 60 ml EVOO + ¼ tsp salt + (LOW only: 1 tsp fresh lemon) → 20 sec / speed 6.
- Make small, use same day (fresh herb blends don't store well for histamine).
- **STRICT flavour toolkit:** salt, EVOO, fresh herbs, fresh ginger, cooked garlic (wk2+), a little grated fresh carrot for sweetness. That's the safe palette.

## Slow cooker (the honest version)

Slow cooking works *against* low-histamine: long cook times + hours held warm both raise histamine. Two ways to handle it:

- **LOW tier — the fresh-and-freeze method:** buy the **freshest** meat, cook the shortest slow-cook that works, and **portion + freeze the instant it finishes** — do NOT hold it on "keep warm," and do NOT fridge it for days.
- **STRICT tier — skip the slow cooker.** Use the pan-sear (recipe 6) or short-cook stock methods instead.

### 9. Fresh lamb shoulder — Slow cooker (LOW tier, freeze-immediately)
**Tier: LOW only.** Swaps for: the plan's slow lamb (which assumed fridge leftovers — that's the histamine problem).

- **Setting: LOW · 4–5 hr** (shortest that pulls apart — not the plan's 6 hr; less time = less histamine).
1. Rub 500 g **very fresh** lamb shoulder with 2 tsp salt, 1 tbsp dried rosemary, 1 tbsp EVOO. (No garlic wk1; cooked garlic OK wk2+.)
2. 100 ml water in base. Cook LOW 4–5 hr until it pulls with 2 forks.
3. **The moment it's done:** pull the meat, portion into single meals, cool fast, **freeze within 30 min.** Do not leave on keep-warm. Do not fridge for the week.
4. Sides: fresh-roast peeled carrot + zucchini (200°C 25 min); steamed green beans. No mint *sauce* (vinegar) — fresh mint + EVOO instead.

### 10. Fresh beef & root-veg stew — Slow cooker (LOW tier, freeze-immediately)
**Tier: LOW only.**
- **Setting: LOW · 4–5 hr.**
1. Cube 250 g **fresh** beef chuck, season with salt.
2. Sear in EVOO (don't crowd), then into slow cooker with 2 chunked carrots + 1 parsnip + ½ swede + 500 ml **fresh short-cook stock** (not long bone broth) + 1 bay leaf + rosemary.
3. LOW 4–5 hr.
4. **Portion + freeze immediately.** Serve over mashed peeled pumpkin (boil 15 min, mash with a little EVOO + salt — skip butter at strict, fine at LOW).

## Breakfast / snacks (all fresh)

- **Eggs** — cooked eggs are low-histamine (avoid raw/undercooked white — mild liberator). Soft-scramble in EVOO, eat fresh. STRICT & LOW safe.
- **Fresh ricotta (recipe 3) + peeled pear** — low-histamine sweet option (pear is one of the safest fruits; avoid banana, citrus, strawberry).
- **Collagen shake** — 10–20 g collagen in water/rice milk. Low-histamine, keeps the protein target.
- **Blueberries** (fresh, small amount) — one of the lower-histamine fruits for LOW tier; strict responders sometimes still react, so test.
