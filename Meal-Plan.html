Foods + habits to AVOID for bloating & gas (your methanogen problem)

Your gut produces too much methane. That's what's causing the bloating, slow transit and excess gas. Methane producers feed on fermentable carbohydrates. Cut these completely in week 1, then reintroduce strategically from week 2 onwards while you watch for symptoms.

Other gut-irritating things to remove entirely

  • Carbonated drinks — sparkling water, soft drinks, kombucha (yes, even kombucha), beer. Direct gas into the gut.
  • Artificial sweeteners — sucralose (Splenda), aspartame, ace-K. Alter microbiome and feed dysbiosis.
  • Sugar alcohols in protein bars — most "low-carb" bars contain maltitol or sorbitol. Wreck your gut.
  • Seed oils — canola, soy, sunflower, safflower, rice bran (in restaurant cooking, processed snacks). Pro-inflammatory, hit mucosal lining.
  • Emulsifiers/thickeners — carrageenan, polysorbate-80, CMC (carboxymethylcellulose), xanthan gum in volume. Damage gut barrier — known to lower sIgA further.
  • Alcohol — already excluded, but worth restating: alcohol directly suppresses sIgA and feeds methanogens.
  • Processed meats with additives — most cheap deli meats contain dextrose, additives, fillers. Stick to whole cuts.
  • Overly processed protein powders — many contain inulin, sucralose, maltitol. Use unflavoured isolate or check the label.

Habits that worsen bloating (just as important as food)

  • Eating too fast — you swallow air. Each bite ≥20 chews. Put fork down between bites.
  • Drinking through a straw — pulls air in.
  • Chewing gum — swallows air + sugar alcohols. Double hit.
  • Talking while eating — air in.
  • Drinking large amounts of water with meals — dilutes stomach acid, slows protein digestion. Sip only. Drink between meals.
  • Lying down after eating — slows gastric emptying, worsens reflux. Wait 2-3 hours.
  • Skipping meals then eating huge ones — overwhelms digestion. Stick to roughly even portions.
  • Not chewing properly — undigested food = more fermentation = more gas.
  • Late-night eating — gut motility slows at night. Last meal 2-3hrs before bed.
  • Cold drinks with hot meals — slows enzyme activity. Room temp or warm with meals.

Pro-motility moves (counter-attack the methane-driven slow transit)

  • 2 kiwifruit daily — clinically proven motility booster (better than psyllium for IMO patients).
  • Magnesium glycinate or citrate at night — already in plan. Citrate is more laxative if you need extra push.
  • Walk after meals — even 10 minutes. Stimulates migrating motor complex.
  • 16:8 intermittent fasting if tolerated — gives the migrating motor complex (MMC) time to clear small intestine. The MMC is what literally sweeps debris through your gut between meals — methanogens thrive when you graze.
  • Ginger tea after meals — prokinetic, reduces bloating.
  • Squat posture for bowel movements — Squatty Potty or footstool. Mechanically straightens the puborectalis sling.
The bloating audit: Bloating that doesn't improve in 2 weeks of strict adherence above means it's pharmacological territory. Allicin (already in the plan) + Atrantil for 4 weeks is the next move. If still no improvement, you need a SIBO breath test (lactulose, 3-hour, methane + hydrogen) via your GP — this measures the actual gas production directly and confirms IMO (intestinal methanogen overgrowth). Treatment for confirmed IMO is rifaximin + neomycin (prescription-only) for 14 days. Diet alone often won't fix entrenched IMO.

8. Caveats & tolerance notes

  • Not medical advice. Run the supplement stack + plan changes by your GP or integrative practitioner, especially with the peptide stack and the eGFR finding.
  • You've paused Reta for lean growth: calories are set to ~2,800 (see macros). Keep the surplus modest and gated on the cystatin C result — a hard bulk on an unconfirmed eGFR, plus EQ's rising haematocrit, is too many stressors at once. Target +0.2–0.35kg/week.

  • Bone broth tolerance: 1 in 5 people don't tolerate bone broth (high glutamate + histamine). If you get headaches, anxiety or jitteriness within 30 min of drinking it, drop to 100ml and watch for symptoms. Some people can't tolerate it at all — sub L-glutamine 5g in water instead.
